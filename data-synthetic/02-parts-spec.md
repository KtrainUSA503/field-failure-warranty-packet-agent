## §3.1 Drive Cylinder Assembly — Overview

The GlideFloor 200 uses a single double-acting hydraulic drive cylinder mounted beneath the
subdeck. The cylinder reciprocates the floor slat groups to convey load. Rated operating
pressure 2,500 psi; rated distributed load **25 tons**.

## §3.2 Drive Cylinder & Seals

The **rod seal (CGF-DCS-114)** prevents hydraulic fluid from escaping past the rod during
the extension stroke. A blown rod seal presents as **external hydraulic fluid leakage at the
rod end** and **loss of slat reciprocation** (the floor stops moving) as pressure bleeds off.

**Covered failure signatures (manufacturing defect):**
- Seal extrusion or lip tear with **clean fluid** and no particulate.
- Failure well within rated load and pressure.

**Non-covered failure signatures (excluded):**
- Scoring of the rod or seal seat from **contaminated fluid** (particulate ingress).
- Seal failure following operation **above 25 tons** or after impact to the rod.

**Required fluid:** ISO VG 46 anti-wear hydraulic oil. Use of non-spec fluid voids seal
coverage.

**Field inspection guidance:** Before removing the failed seal, **photograph the rod end and
the seal in place**, and **record the hour-meter reading** from the control box. Both are
required for warranty submission (policy §3, items 6 and 7).
